Kondagaon a municipality about 70 kilometers from Jagdalpur city is the headquarter of Kondagaon district in the Indian state of Chhattisgarh. Kondagaon separated from Bastar district on 24 January 2012 and formed as 27th District of the Chhattisgarh state. It is mostly renowned for its bell metal craft and other art forms native to the tribal of Bastar. Also known as the Shilp sheher (lit. craft city) of the Chhattisgarh owing to the variety of indigenous crafts produced in the area.

Background

Kondagaon is located at 19.6 ° N 81.67 ° E. [2] It has an average elevation of 593 meters (1945 feet).

 

As of 2001 India census, [3] Kondagaon had a population of 26,772 Males constitute 50% of the population and females 50%. Kondagaon has an average literacy rate of 64%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 73%, and female literacy is 55%. In Kondagaon, 14% of the population is under 6 years of age.

 

Kondagaon lies on the NH 30 highway and can be reached from either Raipur or Jagdalpur. Frequent bus services are available to Kondagaon from Raipur and Jagdalpur. The nearest railway station is Jagdalpur. A helicopter strip has been temporarily constructed in the playground adjacent to the government college campus, which is used occasionally.
