Mau, now known as Maunath Bhanjan, is a town located in Uttar Pradesh, India. This town is the headquarters of the Mau district.

 

Festivals

Common Indian Festivals such as Holi, Diwali, Eid-Ul-Fitar, Eid-Ul-Azha, Muharram, Durga Puja, Vijayadashami, Chhath Puja, Christmas, Bhaiya Duj are celebrated with great pomp and show in the city.

Mau district is one of the districts of Uttar Pradesh state of India, and Mau town is the district headquarters. Mau district is a part of Azamgarh Division. It is situated in the south-eastern part of the state with headquarters in Maunath Bhanjan. The district is surrounded by Ghazipur district on the south, Ballia district in the east, Azamgarh district in the west, and by Gorakhpur and Deoria districts on the north.

Literature

In literature, Mau has produced many great writers and poets, such as:

 

Laxmi Narayan Mishra (1903–1987), born in the village Basti, district Mau. He was a popular play writer of HINDI. He was a theatre personality of Uttar Pradesh. His works became very popular between 1930 and 1950. They were frequently staged by schools, colleges, and amateur groups. Laxmi Narayan Mishra is considered the founder of the problem play in Hindi theatre.

Fiza Ibn-e-Faizi (1923–2009) was a modern Urdu and Persian poet. He was a native of Mau district of Uttar Pradesh and was considered a 'qadir-ul-kalaam' poet and had received numerous awards within the Urdu world. At least, six poetry collections of Faizi had been published and acclaimed. In 2009, a memorial named as Faizi Gate was constructed by the then municipal head near his house.

Maulana Habibur Rahman Azmi (1900–1992) was a well-known Islamic Scholar, belonged to this city. He wrote under pen name of Abul Ma'sir. His works on Islamic Jurisprudence is well acclaimed. He remains a well-known figure in major Islamic seminaries like Medina University, Al-Azhar and Darul-uloom Deoband. His works are cited by Muslims across the world.

Kanhaiya Lal Misra (1903-1975) was Advocate General of Uttar Pradesh, India from 1952 to 1969.

Shyam Narayan Pandey (1907 - 1991) was a Hindi poet famous for his epic poem Haldighati, which is based on the Battle of Haldighati between Akbar and Maharana Pratap.

Dr. Krishna N. Sharma is a prolific writer and researcher, working as the Vice Chancellor at Victoria University, Kampala, Uganda.

4. How to Reach

Location and Boundaries

Mau is located about 315 Kms east of capital Lucknow Uttar Pradesh with two main Highways one National Highway 24 between Gorakhpur (101 kms) and Varanarsi (110 kms) route and on State Highway 34 between Azamgarh (50 kms) and Ballia (85 kms) enroute. It is surrounded by districts – Ballia in the East, Deoria in the North, Azamgarh in the West and Gazipur in the South.

 

Mau is well connected by road and Railway.

 

Roadways:

Frequent road-transport is available for Varanarsi, Azamgarh and Gorakhpur. Direct buses are also available for Lucknow and New Delhi.

 

Online Bus ticket reservation can be book on UPSRTC

 

Trains :

Mau is a railway junction connecting Varanarsi, Gorakhpur, Azamgarh and Chapra. Many trains are available for Gorakhpur, Chapra, Varanarsi, Lucknow, New Delhi, Kolkatta, Patna, Allahabad, Mumbai, Durg, Amritsar.

 

Online Train ticket reservation can be done on IRCTC
