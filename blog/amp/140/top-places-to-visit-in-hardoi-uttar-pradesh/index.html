Hardoi is a city and municipal board in Hardoi district in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is the administrative headquarters of Hardoi district.

 

Demographics

As of 2011 India census, Hardoi city had a population of 1,30,429. Males constitute 52.59% of the population and females 47.41%. In 2011 Average literacy rate of Hardoi city was 81.67% higher than the national average of 74.9%: male literacy was 86.59%, and female literacy was 76.22%. In Hardoi, 12% of the population is under 6 years of age.

The main crops are paddy, sugarcane, wheat, vegetables, pulses and oilseeds. 1. Sarvan Devi Temple

The Sarvan [SHRAVAN] Devi temple on Sandi Road is a shaktipeeth with the mythological history of having been built on the place where the ear [SHRAVAN] of the SATI fell down. Mythologically speaking it an important destination for all interested in Hindu mythology. This place is especially popular among newly-wed couples who come to seek the blessing of SATI for a happy married life. For others tourists, it is a just another temple which with its limited resources lacks the luster of other heavily financed temples. In the same complex, there are other idols too.

2. Sandi Bird Sanctuary

Sandi Bird Sanctuary is situated at a distance of 19 km on Hardoi-Sandi Road in Hardoi district of Uttar Pradesh. Sandi Bird Sanctuary is nearly 1 km. from Sandi town on Main Road, Nawabganj, near Sandi Police Station Sandi (Hardoi). Sandi Bird sanctuary was developed in the year 1990 in view to protecting the natural habitats and aquatic vegetation for the local residents and migratory birds. The Sandi Bird sanctuary is also known by its ancient name as “Dahar Jheel” (Jheel = Lake). The lake is spread over 3.0854 km2 area. River Garra earlier named as Garun Ganga also located near the sanctuary. Migratory birds rest for some time in the riverfront before reaching to Sandi Bird sanctuary. The migratory birds start coming at the beginning of winter in the month of November. As a tourist destination, Sandi is a paradise for bird lovers and is an ideal place for those who want to explore nature. The best time to visit the sanctuary is from December to February. The nearest railway station is at Hardoi (19 km).
